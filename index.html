MISSION TO THE MOON

DATE

  • July 16th 1969

CREW

  • Commander - Neil. A Armstrong.
  • Lunar Module Pilot - Edwin E. 'Buzz' Aldrin.
  • Command Module Pilot - Michael Collins.
    • Click here for the crew bios.

OBJECTIVES

  • Land on the Moon.
  • Deploy T.V camera.
  • Position Laser Ranging Refractor.
  • Install Solar Wind & Seismic Experiments.
  • Return Safely

DETAILS

  • Take off at 09:32am EDT.
  • Enter 'Translunar Injection' (Path to the moon) for 3 days with Television broadcasts made on 17th & 18th.
  • Land on the lunar surface on 20th July 1969, Armstrong saying "Eagle has landed" quote.
  • Armstrong & Aldrin step onto the Moon at 11:00pm EDT, Armstrong uttering the famous, "one small step" quote heard here:
  • Both astronauts collect samples and take photographs of the terrain. Leave an American Flag, patch, to honour the fallen astronauts of Apollo 1 and plaque reading "Here men from the planet Earth first set foot upon the Moon. July 1969 A.D. We came in peace for all mankind.".
  • Return journey on July 22nd at 23:00pm EDT.
  • Landing in Pacific Ocean 8 days after leaving Cape Kennedy Centre.
  • Crew were quarantined for 21 day to protect Earth from possibly outer space germs. Suits and vehicles were created for them to relocate them to a specialised facility.
  • At the request of President Nixon, Apollo 11 crew, family members, NASA officials and State officials went on a World Tour. Visiting 24 countries, meeting all forms of dignitaries in the space of 45 days.
